# Governance

The protocol uses governance so that stakers can influence key decisions. Voting power comes from staked NUMS in the vault—the more you stake, the more influence you have over proposals, treasury spending, and revenue allocation.

## Votes

The **Governor** system manages proposals and votes. Parameters and upgrades can be submitted to community vote.

### Proposal types

Typical proposals include game parameters (burn percentage, game settings), treasury matters (fund allocation, spending, grants), and protocol upgrades (contract upgrades, new features).

### Voting

Voting power is based on **staked NUMS** in the vault (vNUMS balance). Proposals have a voting delay, voting period, and execution delay (timelock). Quorum and approval thresholds are set in the Governor contract.

:::note
**Initial parameters (Governor contract).** At protocol launch:

- Voting delay: 5 minutes
- Voting period: 10 minutes
- Proposal threshold: 1 vNUMS
- Quorum: 30% of circulating vNUMS
:::

### How to participate

First, stake NUMS in the vault. Then review active proposals and cast your vote. Proposals that pass move to timelock before execution.

## Treasury

The **Treasury** centralizes protocol funds and can be controlled or influenced through governance. It holds protocol assets (USDC, NUMS, or other approved tokens), enables fund allocation via governance votes, supports protocol operations and development as an operational reserve, can allocate funds to vault stakers, and backs grants and partnerships approved by the community.

Treasury actions typically require a passed governance proposal, a timelock delay for execution, and controlled execution logic. Funds are only moved when governance approves specific proposals.

## Earnings

The DAO recovers fees linked to the liquidity pool owned by the treasury, which has a 5% swap fee. These fees generate a reserve of funds that accumulates over time.

The DAO also recovers revenues generated by the vault when those revenues are not attributed to account contracts—for example, when vNUMS has been deposited into a liquidity pool and the pool contract becomes eligible for rewards instead of the original depositor. In such cases, the DAO has the power to redirect this value to the treasury, but only after a successful governance vote.
