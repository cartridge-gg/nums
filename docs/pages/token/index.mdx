# Token

The **NUMS** token is the native asset of the game. Players buy games with USDC; the entry is distributed across buy-back and burn, referrals, and protocol revenue. Rewards in NUMS are minted based on performance. This page covers the economy, supply, and price mechanics.

### Estimated supply

An **estimated supply** is defined at the protocol level to determine the supply coefficient (see the [Multiplier](#multiplier) section). This estimate is initially set at 1,000,000 at the time of this document and can be adjusted up or down by the DAO. This value enables, among other things, a maximum supply of approximately 2,000,000 tokens.

### Supply dynamics

Player activity on the game continuously **burns** and **mints** tokens. Over time, community performance is the only criterion that stabilizes the token supply—which remains between 0 and 2,000,000.

### Reward distribution

When the player finishes a game, rewards in NUMS are **newly minted** based on their score and their multiplier (see section below). They are credited immediately to the player's wallet—no manual action is required. Once received, NUMS are fully free to use: stake them, trade them, or hold them—no restrictions apply.

:::note
**Supply impact.** If the player underperforms, supply will have been burned in the process. If the player overperforms, supply will have been created in the process.
:::

## Price

Although the USD value of the token over time is not known in advance, we can nonetheless estimate that its price will depend on protocol revenue. Since you must stake the token to receive a share of protocol revenue (see the [Staking](/staking) section for more details), it is likely—barring euphoria—that the price will be directly correlated to these revenues. The more the protocol is used, the more revenue it generates, and the more attractive it becomes to hold shares in the protocol to capture a portion of that revenue.

:::note
**Initial reference price:** $0.01 per NUMS (see the distribution above for details).
:::
